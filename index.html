New Town Hall

The New Town Hall or New Town Hall in Hanover, Germany, is a city hall that opened on July 20, 1913, after being under construction for 12 years. It is a magnificent castle-like building of the era of Wilhelm II in eclectic style on the southern edge of the inner city.The building is built on 10 hectares. It is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Hanover, where; It is one of the most prominent buildings in the city due to its foundation on a beech wood belt. It is also decorated with a tower in the form of a dome that reaches a height of one hundred meters. Inside the hall includes a group of models of the city, and Hodler's room receives great attention, as; It includes a huge painting called the Oath of Allegiance from 1913.

Hanover Opera

The Opera Building is one of the most important places of tourism in Hanover, along Georgestrasse, and is distinguished by its stunning neoclassical design. It was founded in 1852 and is now operated by the professional State Opera Company. The venue provides opera performances to its fans during the performance season, which runs from September to June.
